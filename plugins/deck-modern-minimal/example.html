CONTEXT

Why this memo

We've spent the quarter removing meetings and adding owners.

The thesis: meetings exist because nobody owns the decision. Once a single person owns it, the meeting becomes a one-line message.

The numbers below say it worked. The next page shows where it didn't.

03 / 08
WHAT CHANGED
04
Three structural moves

The three changes that moved the needle.

01

Single owner per channel

One person makes the call. Anyone with a question writes it down. The owner answers in under 4 hours.

02

Async by default

All standing meetings became channels. Sync only if a real-time decision is required.

03

Visible queue

Every channel shows what's blocked, in days. If anything ages past 7 days, the owner is on the hook publicly.
